I am a Research Assistant at the Urban Analytics Lab, National University of Singapore. My research develops human-centred green space analytics to understand how urban parks and green spaces are described, used, perceived, and assessed.

My work connects landscape architecture with urban data science. I use geospatial analysis, machine learning, natural language processing, large language models, and multimodal urban data to study park features, physical activity, sensory perception, satisfaction, and public health-related outcomes. Much of my recent work uses online reviews, surveys, images, and other urban data sources to quantify park features and experiences, and to support scalable green space assessment.

A central goal of my research is to translate urban data into reliable and actionable evidence for green space planning and design, from within-park design decisions to city-wide strategies.
